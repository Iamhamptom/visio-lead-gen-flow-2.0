## 1. NAAMSA New Vehicle Sales — Full Year 2025

South Africa's new vehicle market staged a decisive recovery in 2025, surpassing pre-pandemic levels for the first time and posting its strongest annual result in more than a decade.

- **Total new vehicle sales 2025: 596,818 units** (up **15.7%** from 516,976 in 2024)
- **Passenger cars: 422,292 units**, up **20.1%** YoY — the standout segment
- Best annual performance in over 10 years
- The recovery is widely attributed to:
  - Cumulative SARB interest rate cuts of **150 basis points** since September 2024
  - Record-low vehicle inflation
  - Improved household liquidity and consumer confidence
  - Aggressive pricing from new Chinese entrants

Vehicle exports lost ground during the year even as domestic sales accelerated, creating a divergence between the manufacturing and retail sides of the industry.

## 3. Top Dealer Groups in South Africa

### Motus Holdings (JSE: MTH) — the giant
Motus is comfortably Southern Africa's largest automotive group.

- **FY2025 group revenue: ~R112.6 billion** (year ended 30 June 2025), down marginally ~1% YoY due to softer international new-vehicle sales (UK Retail Commercial, Australia Retail). A separate aggregator reports R115.6bn — variance likely reflects definitional/segment differences.
- **South African dealerships: 323** (FY2025)
- **South African vehicle sales: 52,548 units in 2025, +8% YoY**
- **H1 FY2026 (six months to 31 Dec 2025): revenue +3% to R57.7bn**, driven by SA volume strength
- Recent reports of staff resistance to a pay-cut programme (Jan 2026) suggest cost pressure despite top-line recovery

### CMH (Combined Motor Holdings, JSE: CMH)
A separate listed dealer group, smaller than Motus, focused on franchised retail and car hire (First Car Rental). Detailed FY2025 figures were not retrieved in this research pass and should be sourced directly from their SENS results before publication.

### Super Group (JSE: SPG)
Listed group with both dealer (SG Dealerships, predominantly VW/Audi/Mercedes) and supply-chain divisions. FY2025 specific dealer-segment numbers were not independently verified in this pass.

### Halfway Group, Barons (Motus), Imperial / Lereko, Hatfield Motor Group, NMI-DSM
Privately held or subsidiary groups with significant regional footprints. No verified consolidated 2025 numbers retrieved in this pass.

## 4. Used Car Market

The used car market remains substantially larger than the new car market by unit volume — and grew faster than new in headline terms in some segments during 2025.

- **Used vehicles sold in 2025: 383,410 units** (per AutoTrader Annual Car Industry Report, 20th edition)
- **Used market value 2025: R160.1 billion**, +**7% YoY**
- AutoTrader's report draws on data from 20+ brands and the platform's >46m users
- **Ratio context:** ~383k used vs 596k new — but the AutoTrader figure measures only what flows through tracked dealer/marketplace channels; the true used market (including private peer-to-peer) is materially larger. Mordor Intelligence and Data Insights Market value the broader used market at **USD 12.9bn–17.9bn (2025)**, with projected ~10% CAGR through 2031–2033 — these are third-party estimates, not industry consensus.
- **Key dynamics 2025:** affordability-driven demand, interest rate cuts boosting financing, online marketplaces (AutoTrader, Cars.co.za) deepening transparency, and rising share of Chinese-brand used stock entering the secondary market for the first time.

## 6. EV Adoption in South Africa

Despite headline noise, **pure battery-electric (BEV) adoption in South Africa remains negligible** in 2025. The growth story is in hybrids and plug-in hybrids ("New Energy Vehicles", NEVs), not pure EVs.

- **BEV sales 2025: 1,018 units** — just **0.17%** of total new vehicle sales
- **BEV sales actually fell 17% YoY** (from 1,231 in 2024)
- However, **H1 2025 BEV sales were +65% YoY**, suggesting late-year softness pulled the full year down
- The broader **NEV category (HEV + PHEV + BEV) roughly doubled 2023→2024** as Chinese entrants flooded the market

### Brands pushing EVs
- **BYD** — 13 dealers in 2025, targeting 20 by year-end and 30–35 by 2026 (≈3× expansion). Recently doubled local lineup with Shark PHEV bakkie, Sealion 6 PHEV crossover, Sealion 7 BEV SUV. Cheapest entry: BYD Dolphin Surf at **R339,900**.
- **GWM** — largest Chinese automaker in SA by volume. GWM Ora 03 (Haval Ora) at ~**R700,000** is the cheapest "mainstream" EV.
- **Volvo** — dominates the **used** EV market: EX30 and XC40 Recharge are the most-listed used EVs in SA (CleanTechnica, Nov 2025).
- **BMW** — continues iX, i4, i5, i7 imports; building i-line locally is under review.
- **Mercedes-Benz, Audi** — premium EVs continue but volumes are tiny.

### Barriers
1. **Price gap:** cheapest EV is ~3.5× the cheapest ICE car (~R200k)
2. **Charging infrastructure:** **<400 public chargers vs ~4,800 petrol stations**, concentrated in Gauteng and Western Cape
3. **Grid instability** (load-shedding legacy) makes home charging less reliable
4. **Import duties + ad-valorem luxury tax** push EV prices up; the long-promised local NEV production incentive has been slow to translate into showroom prices
5. Lack of resale value confidence — though the early Volvo used market is starting to break this

## 7. Recent Significant Moves (2025 → early 2026)

- **BYD network expansion:** ~13 → 20 dealers in 2025, targeting 30–35 by end of 2026. Aggressive product expansion with Shark, Sealion 6, Sealion 7.
- **Chery launching 8 new hybrids in SA**, including 5 EREVs and 3 HEVs, plus two new small crossovers and a forthcoming pickup. Omoda & Jaecoo (premium sub-brands) studying local assembly.
- **GWM hunts SA factory** after losing the local-assembly competition to Chery — exploring outsourcing or SKD plant options.
- **Suzuki overtakes VW Group for #2 brand position** — first time ever, on the back of Swift, Fronx and S-Presso volume.
- **Mahindra breaks into the top 10 brands for the first time**, +40.7% YoY.
- **Nissan collapses 32.3% YoY**, falling out of the top 10 — the worst major-brand performance of the year.
- **Toyota Hilux 13th consecutive year as #1 model** — but with serious pressure from the upcoming new Ranger and surging Chinese double-cabs (BYD Shark, GWM P-Series).
- **Motus pay-cut staff dispute (Jan 2026)** — even with rising SA volumes, the country's largest dealer group is squeezing operating costs.
- **Used EV market emerging** — Volvo EX30 / XC40 Recharge are the leading used-EV listings on AutoTrader/Cars.co.za, hinting at the start of a viable secondary EV market.
