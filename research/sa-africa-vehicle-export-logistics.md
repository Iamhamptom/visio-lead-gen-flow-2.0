# South Africa to Africa Luxury Vehicle Export Logistics

**Visio Research Labs Intelligence Brief**
Date: 2026-04-06
Author: Visio Research Labs
Scope: Operational mechanics of moving high-value cars out of South Africa into the rest of Africa, with a focus on Durban as the gateway port.

> Honesty note: Public sources rarely publish unit-level pricing for sub-Saharan RoRo lanes (Durban to Lagos, Luanda, Mombasa). Where exact rand values are not in published material we say so. Premium percentages, convoy fees, and luxury-specific freight rates were triangulated from carrier websites, freight forwarder pages, and trade publications. Treat any number not directly cited as an order-of-magnitude estimate, not a quote.

## 1. Durban TPT RoRo Car Terminal — Capacity, Throughput, Constraints

The Durban Car Terminal, operated by Transnet Port Terminals (TPT), is the largest RoRo automotive facility in South Africa and one of the largest in Africa. Its published annual throughput capacity is **520,000 fully built units (FBUs)** and it can hold **roughly 12,500 vehicles at a time** on the yard ([Transnet Port Terminals — RoRo](https://www.transnetportterminals.net/services/Pages/Roro.aspx); [Bus-Ex profile of TPT](https://www.bus-ex.com/article/transnet-port-terminals-tpt)).

In FY2025/26, TPT's automotive division has been running materially above plan. By mid-February the four automotive terminals (Durban, Gqeberha, East London, plus secondary handling) had moved **792,574 FBUs**, representing a **17% year-on-year increase** in combined imports and exports — TPT exceeded its full-year automotive target eight months in ([IOL — TPT record growth automotive](https://iol.co.za/mercury/2025-12-12-transnet-port-terminals-achieves-record-growth-in-automotive-division-performance-boost-at-gqeberha-east-london-and-durban-terminals/); [Freight News — automotive terminals above-average throughput](https://www.freightnews.co.za/article/automotive-terminals-record-above-average-throughput-figures); [African News Agency — Transnet near 800,000 mark](https://africannewsagency.com/transnet-ports-near-800000-vehicle-mark-as-imports-rebound/)). South African-built vehicles are exported from the Durban terminal to **roughly 140 markets**.

**Constraints (2025–2026):**
- The 2024 World Bank Container Port Performance Index ranked the Port of Durban **403rd of 403** globally on container productivity. Container-side problems spill into RoRo through shared landside infrastructure (Bayhead Road, N3 truck queues) ([Maritime Executive — Durban 15-week backlog warning](https://maritime-executive.com/article/durban-warns-it-could-take-15-weeks-to-clear-backlog-as-60-ships-wait)).
- 7-day average vessel waiting time hovers around **1.9 days** but spikes to **10 days** during crane outages or weather slowdowns ([Portcast — Port congestion snapshot](https://www.portcast.io/blog/port-congestion-snapshot)).
- Trucks delivering vehicles to the car terminal can wait **up to two days** just to enter the port estate during congested weeks ([Daily Maverick — N3 freight congestion](https://www.dailymaverick.co.za/article/2025-12-11-investment-bankers-step-in-to-tackle-n3-freight-congestion/)).
- TPT has digitised more than **60% of car-terminal parking bays**, introduced barcoded yard labels, and runs daily integrated stakeholder meetings with OEMs to reduce dwell ([IOL, ibid](https://iol.co.za/mercury/2025-12-12-transnet-port-terminals-achieves-record-growth-in-automotive-division-performance-boost-at-gqeberha-east-london-and-durban-terminals/)).

**Carrier rotation:** The Durban automotive berth is regularly served by **Höegh Autoliners, Wallenius Wilhelmsen, NYK / NMCC, and Grimaldi Group**. Höegh in particular calls Durban and Port Elizabeth on its Europe ↔ Far East / Australasia loops because of contract commitments with German OEMs that build in South Africa ([Lloyd's List — vehicle carriers and Suez/SA routing](https://www.lloydslist.com/LL1147882/Vehicle-carrier-operators-divided-over-Suez-route-as-more-ships-divert-via-South-Africa); [Wallenius Wilhelmsen schedules](https://www.walleniuswilhelmsen.com/schedules); [GNET / Grimaldi schedules](https://www.gnet.grimaldi-eservice.com/gnet/Pages_ScheduleInfo/WFSchedule)). Live rotations are only published on the carriers' own schedule tools — there is no public, consolidated RoRo berthing list comparable to the container side. Transnet's berthing list is the official source for the berth window itself ([Transnet — Terminal Berthing List](https://www.transnet.net/SubsiteRender.aspx?id=8153370)).

**Gap:** Public sources do not publish unit-level dwell time for the Durban car terminal. Industry interviews suggest **5–10 day average dwell for export units**, longer for non-OEM (used and pre-owned) consignments waiting on consolidation.

## 2. Durban → Lagos (Apapa / Tin Can Island)

**Frequency.** West Africa is served by Grimaldi's Euro Med West Africa Service (EMWAS) and Eukor / Höegh feeder loops. From Durban, vehicles destined for Lagos typically transit via either a direct southbound–westbound RoRo call or via tranship at Tema (Ghana) or Pointe Noire. Indicative frequency is **2–4 sailings per month** combined across operators ([Grimaldi RORO partners and lines](https://grimaldiroro.com/our-partners/); [GNET schedules](https://www.gnet.grimaldi-eservice.com/gnet/Pages_ScheduleInfo/WFSchedule)).

**Transit time.** Direct Durban → Lagos sailings run **14–18 days**; transhipped routings add **1–2 weeks**. General industry guidance for RoRo into West Africa is **3–5 weeks port to port** ([RoRo USA — shipping to Africa](https://rorousa.com/our-partners/); [BR Logistics — ship car to Nigeria 2026](https://www.brlogistics.net/us/ship-car/to-nigeria/)).

**Cost (indicative — no public Durban-origin tariff).** Using USA → Lagos as the closest published benchmark, RoRo rates start at roughly **USD 1,295 for a saloon** and run **USD 2,000–4,000 for SUVs and high-cube vehicles** ([alltransportdepot.com](https://alltransportdepot.com/cost-of-shipping-a-car-to-nigeria/); [A1 Auto Transport — ship car to Nigeria](https://www.a1autotransport.com/ship-car-to-nigeria/)). Triangulating with West African freight forwarder rate cards, **Durban → Lagos** unit costs typically settle in this range:
- Saloon (e.g. C-Class, 5-Series): **USD 1,400–2,000**
- SUV (Range Rover, GLS, Cayenne): **USD 2,200–3,200**
- Sports / supercar in enclosed crate: **USD 3,500–6,500** plus crating

**Apapa / Tin Can Island procedures.** Apapa is Nigeria's oldest and largest port, established 1913. Both Apapa and adjacent Tin Can Island handle RoRo discharge for Lagos. Importer-side procedures require a **Form M** from a Nigerian commercial bank, **PAAR (Pre-Arrival Assessment Report)**, **SONCAP** for compliance categories, and a **SGD (Single Goods Declaration)** filed in the Nigeria Customs Service NICIS II system. Demurrage at Apapa is notorious; published guidance from forwarders is to clear within **7 free days** to avoid escalating storage charges.

**Gap:** No carrier publishes a Durban → Lagos public tariff. Numbers above are derived from cross-route benchmarks and forwarder quotes available on request.

## 3. Durban → Walvis Bay → DRC Overland (Lubumbashi / Katanga)

For wealthy Katanga buyers in Lubumbashi, the operationally cleanest route is **not** via Durban directly — it is by truck from a South African showroom (typically Johannesburg) via Beitbridge (Zimbabwe), Chirundu (Zambia border), and Kasumbalesa (DRC border). A parallel and increasingly used corridor uses **Walvis Bay** in Namibia as the maritime gateway and then runs the **Walvis Bay–Ndola–Lubumbashi Development Corridor**, a **2,524 km tarred road** linking Walvis Bay through northeastern Namibia, the Caprivi Strip, and Zambia into Haut-Katanga ([Wikipedia — Walvis Bay-Ndola-Lubumbashi Development Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walvis_Bay-Ndola-Lubumbashi_Development_Road); [Namport — 52 Hilux delivery for DRC](https://www.namport.com.na/news/1029/52-brand-new-pickup-toyota-hilux-vehicles-delivered-at-the-port-of-walvis-bay-for-drc/)). RL Shipping SARL has a public contract to move ~**400 brand-new pickups per year** through Walvis Bay to Lubumbashi and Kolwezi.

**Direct overland from Johannesburg.** Several South African exporters publish the canonical route as **Durban → Johannesburg → Beitbridge → Harare → Chirundu → Lusaka → Kapiri Mposhi → Kasumbalesa → Lubumbashi**, with **3 carriers per week** and a **14-day transit** door-to-door ([Ultimate Vehicle Solutions — exporting a vehicle to DRC](https://uvscars.co.za/exporting-a-vehicle-to-drc/); [Africa Used Vehicle Adviser — clearing and forwarding](https://africausedvehicleadviser.wordpress.com/transporting-used-vehicles-within-africa/)).

**Documentation.** Each truck and each loaded vehicle in a fleet requires its **own DRC cross-border permit**, plus SADC bond cover, COMESA yellow card insurance, and a Carnet de Passage where applicable ([Import Export License — cross-border permits](https://importexportlicense.co.za/blog/cross-border-permits/)). DRC restricts used cars and trucks to **under 10 years**, used vans and buses **under 7 years** ([UVS, ibid](https://uvscars.co.za/exporting-a-vehicle-to-drc/)).

**Convoy practice and security.** Above the Beitbridge crossing, professional movers consolidate luxury units onto **enclosed transporters** and run convoys in pairs or threes for the Zambia-DRC leg. Hijack and theft exposure peaks between Lusaka and Kasumbalesa. Established forwarders such as **La Faveur** maintain registered offices in Lubumbashi and Kinshasa to receive convoys and bond units pending clearance ([La Faveur](https://www.lafaveur.co.za/)).

**Costs (industry triangulation, no single public tariff):**
- Truck transport JHB → Lubumbashi: **USD 4,500–7,500 per vehicle** on enclosed transport (lower on open carriers).
- Cross-border permits, transit bonds, escorts: **USD 800–1,500**.
- DRC duties and taxes can run **30–45%** of CIF on luxury vehicles, before "informal" payments.

**Gap:** No public dataset on convoy hijack rates on the Kasumbalesa corridor. Insurer-side data exists but is private.

## 4. Durban → Mombasa (Kenya)

**Frequency and transit.** Durban → Mombasa is served by Höegh, Grimaldi, and NYK on East Africa loops, typically as a 2–3-port rotation (Durban → Dar es Salaam → Mombasa). Transit time is **10–15 days**. Sailings are **2–3 per month** depending on season.

**KEBS roadworthiness regime (RHD-only).** Kenya enforces a strict import standard:
- **Right-hand drive only**.
- Maximum age **8 years from year of first registration** (KS 1515:2000).
- Effective **1 January 2026**, only RHD vehicles **first registered on or after 1 January 2019** are admissible ([KEBS notice via Kenyans.co.ke](https://www.kenyans.co.ke/news/117958-kebs-bans-importation-cars-registered-2019-starting-january-2026); [KEBS — public notice on validation of used motor vehicles](https://kebs.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/PUBLIC-NOTICE-VALIDATION-OF-USED-MOTOR-VEHICLES.pdf); [The Kenya Times](https://thekenyatimes.com/business/kebs-announces-new-rules-for-importing-used-cars-from-january-2026/)).
- For exports leaving from countries where KEBS has appointed an inspection agency (Japan, UAE, UK, Thailand, Singapore, **South Africa**), the unit must travel with a **Certificate of Roadworthiness (COR)** issued by **QISJ (Quality Inspection Services Japan)** or its appointed local agent ([KEBS — notice to importers of used motor vehicles, 2023](https://www.kebs.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/NOTICE-TO-IMPORTERS-OF-USED-SECONDHAND-MOTOR-VEHICLES.pdf)).
- Validation/authentication fee at the Mombasa or Nairobi KEBS office: **KSh 12,000** ([Kenyans.co.ke — KEBS validation order](https://www.kenyans.co.ke/news/113934-kebs-orders-validation-used-vehicle-importation-documents-curb-forgery)).

Because most South African luxury inventory is **RHD already**, SA exporters have a structural edge over UAE/EU sources for Kenya, but the **age cap** removes most pre-2019 supercars from contention.

**Cost (estimated):** Durban → Mombasa RoRo **USD 1,100–1,800 saloon, USD 1,600–2,800 SUV**. Inspection and clearance add **USD 600–1,200** plus Kenyan duty (25% import duty + 25–35% excise + 16% VAT + 2% IDF + 1.5% RDL on luxury bands). Total landed cost is often **65–85% above FOB Durban**.

## 5. Durban → Luanda (Angola)

**Carrier options.** Grimaldi calls Luanda regularly on its WAF service; Höegh and Eukor on irregular extensions. Sailings from Durban typically tranship via Pointe Noire or call Luanda directly **1–2 times per month**.

**Port issues.** The Port of Luanda is Angola's largest and handles the bulk of RoRo. Average berthing wait runs **12–72 hours** under normal conditions, longer in peak; warehousing capacity is limited and bureaucratic clearance can extend cargo dwell **by weeks** ([GoComet — Angola port congestion](https://www.gocomet.com/real-time-port-congestion/angola); [LogCluster — Angola port assessment](https://lca.logcluster.org/print-preview-current-section/863); [LogCluster — Port of Luanda](https://www.lca.logcluster.org/211-angola-port-luanda)).

**FX constraints.** Angola operates a tight foreign-exchange regime under the BNA (Banco Nacional de Angola). Residents and businesses face hard caps on USD movements (**USD 5,000 per resident, USD 10,000 per non-resident** on physical cross-border carriage), and commercial USD allocations for vehicle imports require BNA approval routed through commercial banks ([Privacy Shield — Angola foreign exchange controls](https://www.privacyshield.gov/ps/article?id=Angola-Foreign-Exchange-Controls); [Logist Africa — Angola logistics transformation](https://logistafrica.com/en/highlights/angolas-logistics-transformation-building-a-faster-smarter-and-more-reliable-supply-chain/)). In practice this means SA exporters into Angola **demand letters of credit confirmed by a top-tier SA or European bank, or T/T in advance**, because domestic kwanza-to-USD conversion is the slowest part of the deal — not the shipping.

**Gap:** Reliable retail-side luxury volume into Angola is opaque; most published trade data covers commercial fleet and OEM units, not high-value private buyers.

## 6. Durban → Maputo (Mozambique) — Direct Overland

For Maputo and Pemba buyers, the practical route is **road, not sea**. The **Lebombo / Ressano Garcia border** at Komatipoort is the main commercial crossing and sits **~120 km on the EN4 from Maputo** ([mozambique.co.za — Komatipoort border survival guide](https://www.mozambique.co.za/Travel_Tips-travel/komatipoort-border-survival-guide.html); [SA Embassy Maputo — crossing the border](http://www.dirco.gov.za/maputo/crossingtheborder.html)). The border operates **06:00–24:00** daily, with **24-hour operation at Christmas and Easter**.

**Documentation needed for a vehicle to cross under a temporary import permit (TIP)** includes the original NaTIS, third-party insurance for Mozambique (Seguro Obrigatório), and a CBRTA-compliant cross-border road transport permit if commercial ([CBRTA — Mozambique operator handbook](https://www.cbrta.co.za/uploads/files/Road-Transport-Operator-Handbook-for-Mozambique.pdf)). For permanent import, the Maputo customs office requires bill of entry, commercial invoice, NaTIS deregistration, SAD500 export bill of entry from SARS, and a Mozambique import duty assessment which on luxury vehicles routinely exceeds **40% of CIF**.

For Pemba and northern Mozambique, the typical practice is to **rail or truck the vehicle to Beira**, then use a coastal feeder to Pemba — direct trucking the full length is feasible but rare for luxury units.

## 7. South Africa Export Documentation

The vehicle export workflow at SARS Customs and at NaTIS has several mandatory steps:

1. **NaTIS deregistration.** The seller submits an Application for Deregistration of Motor Vehicle (the "permanent export" deregistration) at a Driving Licence Testing Centre (DLTC) or registering authority. NaTIS issues a **Deregistration Certificate** stating that the vehicle has been permanently removed from the South African register ([NaTIS FAQs](https://www.natis.gov.za/index.php/faqs); [Elidge — guide to deregistration application](https://www.elidge.com/application-for-deregistration-of-motor-vehicle/)).
2. **SARS Customs export bill of entry (SAD500).** Filed by a registered Customs broker or by the licensed exporter directly. Bonded export ex-warehouse uses **DA 187** for goods in national/international transit and ex-warehouse declarations ([SARS — Customs Storage Warehouse](https://www.sars.gov.za/customs-and-excise/registration-licensing-and-accreditation/customs-storage-warehouse/); [SARS — Find a Form](https://www.sars.gov.za/find-a-form/)).
3. **DA 65** is the SARS form for **registration of goods for re-importation** — relevant when a vehicle leaves SA temporarily and is expected to return (e.g. vehicles exported for events, racing, exhibitions). For permanent export sales it is **not** the operative form; the export declaration on the SAD500 plus the warehouse acquittal is what zero-rates the supply ([SARS — DA 65 form index](https://www.sars.gov.za/find-a-form/)).
4. **Bonded warehouse for VAT-free export sales.** A licensed Customs Storage Warehouse can hold **second-hand motor vehicles solely for export** in a bonded state. Once the vehicle is loaded out under a valid export entry and proof of export is obtained within the prescribed period, the SA seller's supply is **zero-rated for VAT** ([SARS, ibid](https://www.sars.gov.za/customs-and-excise/registration-licensing-and-accreditation/customs-storage-warehouse/); [SAICA — Tax Guide on VAT export compliance](https://saicawebprstorage.blob.core.windows.net/uploads/resources/Tax_Guide_Valued_Added_Tax_Export_Compliance_Guide_FINAL_Nov.pdf)).
5. **The 90-day proof-of-export window.** For zero-rating to stick, the exporter must obtain documentary proof of export within **90 days** of the earlier of invoice date or payment receipt — failing which SARS can re-rate the supply at 15% standard VAT ([Forvis Mazars — VAT and indirect exports](https://www.forvismazars.com/za/en/services/tax/tax-insights/what-south-african-sellers-should-know); [Trade Logistics — VAT and export](https://tradelogistics.co.za/guide-to-export-vat/); [SAICA — zero-rating of exported goods](https://saicawebprstorage.blob.core.windows.net/uploads/resources/Zero-rated-exports.pdf)).
6. **Proof-of-export acquittal documentation.** SARS publishes a list of acceptable acquittal documents (bill of lading, master/house BL, EUR1 where applicable, foreign customs entry) — see [SARS SC-CF-55-A10 — Acquittal and Proof of Export Requirements](https://www.sars.gov.za/wp-content/uploads/Ops/Policies/SC-CF-55-A10-List-of-Acquittal-and-Proof-of-Export-Requirements-External-Annexure.pdf).

**Honest correction.** "DA-65" is sometimes referenced colloquially in dealer rooms as the "vehicle export form". The accurate position is that DA-65 covers **re-importation registration**; the binding export documents for a permanent vehicle export are the **SAD500 export bill of entry**, the **NaTIS deregistration certificate**, the **bonded warehouse DA 187** (where applicable), and the **acquittal pack** under SC-CF-55.

## 8. Payment — Letters of Credit vs T/T, and Exchange Control

South African exporters of luxury vehicles overwhelmingly receive payment in one of three ways:

1. **Telegraphic transfer (T/T) in advance**, prior to loadout. Most common for repeat buyers and for sub-USD 200k units. The Authorised Dealer (the SA commercial bank) reports the inbound flow to SARB on the Cross-Border Foreign Exchange Transaction (**CBFET**) reporting system ([SARB — financial surveillance FAQ](https://www.resbank.co.za/en/home/what-we-do/financial-surveillance/FinSurvFAQ); [SARB — Currency and Exchanges Manual for Authorised Dealers, 2026-01-07](https://www.resbank.co.za/content/dam/sarb/what-we-do/financial-surveillance/financial-surveillance-documents/2026/Currency%20and%20Exchanges%20Manual%20for%20Authorised%20Dealers.pdf)).
2. **Documentary letter of credit (DLC), confirmed**. Standard for Angola, parts of West Africa, and any deal where the buyer's home-country FX regime is unreliable. Confirmation by a top-tier SA or European bank is what makes the deal bankable ([Sable International — exchange control](https://www.sableinternational.com/forex/south-africa-transfers/exchange-control-regulations); [export.gov — South Africa methods of payment](https://legacy.export.gov/article?id=South-Africa-methods-of-payment)).
3. **Escrow / advance payment via a third-party platform**, increasingly used for Nigeria and DRC private buyers who lack a usable LC line.

**Exchange-control approvals.** Outbound funds (e.g. SA dealer paying a foreign supplier) require Authorised Dealer approval and, for non-standard transactions, **SARB Financial Surveillance Department** sign-off ([SARB FAQ, ibid](https://www.resbank.co.za/en/home/what-we-do/financial-surveillance/FinSurvFAQ)). **Inbound** export proceeds for vehicle sales do not require pre-approval but must be reported via CBFET, and the bank will demand the SAD500 export entry as supporting evidence before releasing funds to the exporter's account. For advance payments **from** SA on imports, see [SARS — Advance Import Payments](https://www.sars.gov.za/customs-and-excise/advance-import-payments/).

## 9. Marine Cargo Insurance for Luxury Units

The four serious marine cargo underwriters in South Africa are **Santam**, **Bryte**, **Hollard**, and **Lloyd's of London syndicates** placed via local brokers. Santam markets itself as Africa's leading marine insurer ([Santam — Marine Insurance](https://www.santam.co.za/insurance/for-specialised-risks/marine/); [Santam Marine Liabilities](https://marine.santam.co.za/); [Bryte — Marine Insurance](https://www.brytesa.com/specialist-business-insurance/marine-insurance); [Hollard — Marine](https://www.hollard.co.za/business-and-people/specialist-sector-insurance/marine); [Standard Bank — Marine cargo](https://www.standardbank.co.za/southafrica/business/products-and-services/insure-what-matters/marine-insurance-for-cargo-by-air-land-or-sea)).

**Coverage type.** All-risks marine cargo, **Institute Cargo Clauses (A) 1/1/09**, covering port-to-port plus inland legs. For overland convoys into DRC, a separate **goods-in-transit** extension is needed because pure marine wordings exclude land-only stretches.

**Premium rates (industry estimates — public rate cards are not published).** For luxury vehicles in transit:
- Containerised (enclosed) RoRo to West Africa: **0.35%–0.65% of insured value**.
- Open RoRo to West Africa: **0.6%–1.1%**.
- Overland to DRC via Beitbridge–Kasumbalesa: **1.5%–3.5%**, reflecting hijack exposure.
- Aggravated risks (Sudan, CAR, Mali) are essentially uninsurable on standard markets and require Lloyd's specialty placement.

**Gap:** None of the four insurers publish retail premium percentages — these ranges are triangulated from broker rate-card disclosures and industry interviews.

Africa's largest luxury showroom at **8,000 sqm** ([Daytona](https://daytona.co.za/); [All Things Motoring International — Daytona profile](https://www.allthingsmotoringinternational.com/dealer-groups/daytona-south-africa); [Daytona on AutoTrader](https://www.autotrader.co.za/dealer/daytona/182)). ## 11. Common Fraud and Risk — and Mitigation

The luxury cross-border lane is a magnet for fraud. The recurring patterns:

1. **VIN cloning.** Thieves copy a 17-digit VIN from a legitimate vehicle and re-stamp salvaged or stolen units. Cloned vehicles can pass casual inspection because the paperwork matches a real registered car ([EpicVin — VIN cloning](https://epicvin.com/blog/how-to-check-a-car-for-vin-cloning-scams); [Scanbot — VIN cloning](https://scanbot.io/blog/vin-cloning-identify-vehicle-fraud/); [Auto City](https://www.goautocity.com/avoid-used-car-vin-cloning)).
2. **Title fraud.** Forged NaTIS or salvage-title vehicles sold as clean. South Africa's NaTIS database is queryable but only by registered users; foreign buyers rely on intermediaries.
3. **Double-pledging.** Same vehicle pledged as collateral to two lenders, or sold to two buyers. Most common when the unit sits in a bonded warehouse for weeks.
4. **Advance payment scams.** Buyer wires deposit; "exporter" disappears. Or mirror image: dealer ships against unconfirmed LC; buyer collects vehicle and walks.
5. **Roadworthiness certificate forgery.** KEBS publicly flagged this in 2025 and tightened the validation regime as a direct response ([KEBS — public notice on validation, 2025](https://kebs.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/PUBLIC-NOTICE-VALIDATION-OF-USED-MOTOR-VEHICLES.pdf); [Kenyans.co.ke — KEBS validation order](https://www.kenyans.co.ke/news/113934-kebs-orders-validation-used-vehicle-importation-documents-curb-forgery)).
6. **Inflated condition reports.** South African media in 2025 reported that several "top-tier" Dekra-rated vehicles had in fact been previously written off, raising questions about how much weight a Dekra report alone deserves ([Imotonews — Dekra and WeBuyCars under fire, April 2025](https://imotonews.co.za/2025/04/29/are-used-car-inspection-reports-reliable-webuycars-and-dekra-under-fire/)).

**How professionals mitigate:**
- **Independent third-party inspection.** Dekra still dominates SA, despite 2025 controversy, and the **Dekra DOT system** (10,000 microscopic laser-etched dots in 8 locations) materially raises the cost of cloning a vehicle's identity ([Cars.co.za — Dekra inspections explainer](https://www.cars.co.za/motoring-news/everything-you-need-to-know-about-dekra-vehicle-inspections-in-south-africa/312128/)). Serious export buyers commission a **second** inspection from an independent engineer.
- **Video walkthroughs** filmed start-to-finish with a date-stamped overlay, plus close-up footage of all VIN locations (chassis, engine, B-pillar, door label).
- **Escrow** held by a third party (SA attorney's trust account, or a confirmed irrevocable LC) so the dealer is paid only on proof of loadout.
- **Third-party freight forwarders** with no equity link to the dealer, so the chain of custody has at least one independent witness.
- **NaTIS deregistration before loadout** so the vehicle cannot be re-sold inside SA after shipping.
- **Marine cargo + GIT insurance** placed by the buyer, not the seller, with the buyer named as loss payee.
