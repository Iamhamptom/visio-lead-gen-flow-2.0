## 1. Market Structure — five overlapping sub-markets pretending to be one

"Coaches, trainers and online experts" is not a single market in South Africa; it is five distinct sub-markets that share demand-side buyers but operate on different regulatory, pricing, and delivery models.

- **Professional coaches** — executive coaches, business coaches, leadership coaches, life coaches. Voluntarily affiliated to **COMENSA (Coaches and Mentors Association of South Africa)**, which is SA&apos;s professional body for coaching. COMENSA accreditation levels mirror the **International Coaching Federation (ICF)** ACC, PCC and MCC tiers. Typical engagement: R1,500–R5,000 per session, 6–12 session packages.
- **Personal trainers and fitness coaches** — in-person personal trainers attached to gyms (Virgin Active, Planet Fitness, Kauai-attached studios), boutique studio trainers, and independent trainers offering in-home or park sessions. Typical rate: R350–R800 per session, with package discounts. Largely unregulated beyond voluntary REPSSA (Register of Exercise Professionals of South Africa) accreditation.
- **Online course creators** — SA-based creators selling their own courses via Gumroad, Teachable, Kajabi, Thinkific, or direct-on-platform. Categories include business, marketing, creative skills, finance, fitness, and increasingly AI/prompt engineering. Highly fragmented, no central registry.
- **Tutors and academic support** — online and in-person tutoring, test prep, university support. **TeachMe2** is the dominant SA tutoring marketplace; other operators include Turito, Superprof SA, and a long tail of indie tutors operating via WhatsApp and word of mouth.
- **Wellness and therapy-adjacent practitioners** — nutritionists, dietitians, mental-health coaches (distinct from HPCSA-registered psychologists), yoga instructors, meditation teachers, breathwork practitioners. Regulation varies: dietitians register with HPCSA, most others operate under voluntary professional bodies or not at all.

### Real SA operators and platforms

- **COMENSA (Coaches and Mentors Association of South Africa)** — voluntary professional body for coaches
- **REPSSA (Register of Exercise Professionals)** — voluntary register for fitness professionals
- **Discovery Vitality** — platform-level integration with partner gyms (Virgin Active, Planet Fitness) and partner personal trainers
- **Virgin Active South Africa** — the largest health-club chain in SA, employer and platform for a large number of SA personal trainers
- **Planet Fitness** — the second-largest national health-club chain
- **TeachMe2** — SA&apos;s leading tutoring marketplace (real, well-established)
- **GetSmarter (2U)** — SA-founded online education company acquired by 2U in 2017, operates in the premium short-course market with university partnerships
- **Red &amp; Yellow Creative School of Business** — in-person + online creative skills education

## 2. Market-sizing — the most under-documented vertical in the Visio Lead Gen set

Unlike insurance, medical aid, or home loans &mdash; where regulators and industry bodies publish formal annual industry reports &mdash; the coaching and online expert market in SA has **no authoritative single-source market size**. Any figure in this report should be treated as an informed estimate rather than a cited fact.

- The **global coaching industry** was valued at approximately **USD 4.5 billion in 2023** per the **ICF 2023 Global Coaching Study** (a real, regularly published industry report). Applying a rough pro-rata based on SA&apos;s share of global professional services revenue produces an SA-level estimate in the **R800m &ndash; R2bn** range for professional coaching alone. This is a VRL estimate and not a published figure.
- The **SA fitness industry** &mdash; including health clubs, PT services, and boutique studios &mdash; is widely cited in trade press at roughly **R8 billion annually**, with personal training typically representing a modest fraction of the total club-level revenue. This is a trade-press-level figure, not an audited one.
- The **SA online learning market** &mdash; including everything from tutoring to university-backed short courses to creator-led courses &mdash; is widely estimated in the **R3 &ndash; R5 billion** range, heavily weighted to university-partnered providers like GetSmarter at the top of the pyramid.
- **The combined "coaches, trainers, and online experts" addressable market** in SA is therefore plausibly in the **R10 &ndash; R15 billion range** (VRL informed estimate). This figure is directional only.

**VRL caveat**: market sizing in this vertical is the weakest in the entire Visio Lead Gen set. Any future VRL paper version that quotes a specific SA coaching market size must cite a specific source (COMENSA, ICF, or a formal published industry survey) and flag the estimate explicitly.

## 3. Post-pandemic demand and the remote-work tailwind

The single largest structural shift affecting the SA coaching and online expert market is the post-pandemic normalisation of remote work and the resulting demand for 1:1 accountability, skills transformation, and career transition support.

- **Executive coaching demand** rose materially through 2021-2024 as corporates invested in leadership development for remote-management cultures. This is widely cited in trade press and backed by ICF global membership growth, though SA-specific data is thin.
- **Business and startup coaching** has become a crowded sub-segment, driven by the SA creator-economy emergence and by the high founder mortality rate (most SA startups fail within 24 months of launch, producing a structural demand for "founder coaching" at the moment of pivot).
- **Career transition coaching** has risen in visibility as the two-pot retirement system and the broader cost-of-living squeeze have pushed mid-career professionals to reconsider their work arrangements.
- **Fitness coaching** shifted materially toward hybrid (in-person + app-based accountability) models through 2022-2024, with Discovery Vitality reinforcing the structural overlap between medical scheme wellness incentives and personal training.
- **Online course consumption** has matured in SA, with the creator economy producing genuine SA-based successful course operators (finance content, marketing, creative skills), though the market remains dominated by international platforms (Udemy, Coursera, Skillshare, MasterClass) for the largest share of spend.

**VRL thesis**: the post-pandemic normalisation is a slow structural tailwind, not a spike. A signal layer in this vertical benefits from compounding demand over 3-5 years rather than from a single catalyst event.

## 4. The pricing asymmetry — premium vs discount ends of the market

Unlike most consumer markets where pricing clusters around a central tendency, the SA coaches and experts market is structurally bimodal, with a thin middle.

| Tier | Typical engagement | Buyer | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Premium (R2,000+ / session)** | Executive coaching, HNW business coaching, university-partnered certificates | Corporate-sponsored or HNW self-pay | ICF PCC/MCC coaches, GetSmarter short courses (R15k–R80k per programme) |
| **Mid (R800–R2,000 / session)** | Professional business coaches, specialist fitness trainers, senior tutors | Self-pay professionals | Independent business coaches, TeachMe2 senior tutors |
| **Mid-affordable (R300–R800 / session)** | Entry-level business coaches, junior PTs, general tutors | Self-pay middle income | Virgin Active PTs, junior TeachMe2 tutors |
| **Affordable (R80–R300 / session or course)** | Online creator courses, recorded webinars, affordable tutoring | Self-pay aspirational | Gumroad / Teachable creators, low-fee tutors |

**Implications for lead pricing**: the lead value per engagement varies wildly across tiers. A premium executive coaching lead that converts to a R60,000 six-month engagement is worth more than a R800/month fitness trainer lead &mdash; a 10&ndash;30x spread in lead value within the same vertical. A credible signal layer must segment by tier before any other routing decision.

- Practitioner published rate sheets (COMENSA member listings, TeachMe2 platform data, GetSmarter course catalogue)

## 5. The discovery problem — why this vertical needs a signal layer

The central problem in the SA coaches and experts market is a **discovery problem**, not a demand problem. Buyers in need of a coach, trainer, or expert cannot reliably find the right practitioner because:

1. **No central registry** exists. COMENSA, REPSSA and other voluntary bodies each list a subset of practitioners, with incomplete cross-coverage.
2. **Credential noise** is severe. The term "coach" is unregulated; anyone can call themselves a life coach, business coach, or wellness coach without any training. ICF and COMENSA accreditation exists but is voluntary and inconsistently disclosed.
3. **Price opacity** is the norm. Most coaches and online experts do not publish prices, forcing buyers into a discovery call before learning whether a practitioner is affordable.
4. **Outcome uncertainty** is structural. Unlike insurance (clear claim) or home loans (clear approval), coaching and training outcomes are qualitative and lagged, making comparison-shopping almost impossible.
5. **Word-of-mouth dominance** is strong but breaks down at scale. A buyer whose social network does not include people who have used a specific coach type has no reliable way to find one.

**VRL thesis**: this is the clearest **discovery-layer-needed** vertical in the entire Visio Lead Gen set. Unlike insurance or bond origination where the regulator solves part of the discovery problem, coaching and online expertise has no structural discovery infrastructure. A signal layer that matches buyer intent to practitioner credentials and tier is filling a genuine market gap.

## 6. Regulation — voluntary bodies and the scope-of-practice grey zone

Coaches and online experts in South Africa are largely unregulated, with a patchwork of voluntary professional bodies and a few formal regulatory intersections.

- **COMENSA** &mdash; voluntary professional body for coaches, affiliated internationally to ICF. COMENSA operates a code of ethics, a grievance procedure, and accreditation tiers.
- **ICF** &mdash; international professional body for coaches. ACC / PCC / MCC credentials are internationally recognised.
- **REPSSA** &mdash; voluntary register for fitness professionals.
- **HPCSA** &mdash; regulates healthcare professionals, including dietitians and psychologists. Coaches, trainers, and online experts who stray into HPCSA-scoped clinical territory (mental health therapy, prescribed nutrition, exercise prescription for medical conditions) may be operating outside their legal scope.
- **POPIA** &mdash; applies to all practitioners who hold client personal information, which is effectively all of them.
- **Consumer Protection Act** &mdash; governs service delivery claims and advertising representations.
- **ASA / ARB Code of Advertising Practice** &mdash; governs all advertising including claims of results, testimonials, and before/after transformations.

**Critical scope-of-practice note**: mental health "coaches" who offer anything resembling psychological therapy are operating in a dangerous grey zone. Visio Coach treats this as an explicit routing constraint &mdash; buyers showing mental-health-related intent are routed to HPCSA-registered psychologists, not to unregistered coaches, as a POPIA-compliant consent-gated safety net.
