## 1. How every ARR number is built

Every Visio Lead Gen ARR target follows the same two-line formula:

```
Steady-state ARR = (monthly qualified leads × average lead price × 12)
                 + (panel members × monthly subscription × 12)
```

The numbers in each paper represent the **Year 3–5 steady-state target**, not the Year-1 realistic number. Reaching steady-state requires the full go-to-market build-out: organic content compounding, partner-channel embeds, agent panel activation, paid acquisition, and word-of-mouth flywheel — all of which mature over 18–36 months from launch.

## 2. Ramp schedule — Year 1 → Year 5

For honest planning, every steady-state ARR number must be paired with a ramp schedule. The typical ramp for a signal-driven lead aggregator in the SA market:

| Year | % of steady-state | Drivers |
|---|---:|---|
| Year 1 | **10–15%** | Launch traction, early panel signups, paid acquisition seeding organic |
| Year 2 | **25–35%** | Organic compound begins, first paying customer case studies, panel expansion |
| Year 3 | **45–55%** | Content-driven organic becomes primary, panel fee increases, cross-vertical referral activation |
| Year 4 | **70–85%** | Network effect matures, subscription renewals compound, channel partnerships deliver |
| Year 5+ | **100%** | Steady-state; further growth from new verticals, geography, or adjacent products |

### Why Estate and Shield were over-stated

- **Visio Estate (Commercial Property)**: the original R65M assumed 18,000 annual qualified commercial property leads, but SA commercial property decisions number in the low thousands per year. No plausible capture rate produces 18,000 annual leads in this market. Revised to R30M with a panel subscription emphasis (~R18M subscription + ~R12M lead fees). A signal layer in commercial property is more like an intelligence product than a high-volume lead engine — the revenue model reflects that.
- **Visio Shield (Short-Term Insurance)**: the original R80M assumed 120,000 annual qualified insurance leads, which required near-total capture of all renewal-window shopping intent across the ~629-broker Gauteng market. Revised to R45M at a more realistic 55,000 annual leads (~55% haircut on volume, same pricing).

## 7. How to use this addendum

For any paper-level publication, investor presentation, or client pitch:

1. **Quote the steady-state ARR figure** from the individual paper, always paired with the ramp schedule from Section 2 of this addendum. Never present steady-state as Year-1 guidance.
2. **Cross-reference every key figure** to at least one of the government, banking, or big-player sources in Sections 4–6 before publication. Where a figure cannot be cross-referenced, flag it as a VRL estimate in the paper itself.
3. **Where this addendum differs from the paper** (e.g. Estate R30M revised vs R65M original, Shield R45M revised vs R80M original), the addendum figure is authoritative. The papers will be updated in a rolling revision cycle.
4. **Honesty protocol**: never cite an estimated figure as a verified figure. Never present a VRL thesis as a market consensus. Always disclose the source.
