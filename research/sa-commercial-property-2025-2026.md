## 1. Market Structure — globals, indies, and the REIT counterparty layer

South Africa's commercial property brokerage market is a three-tier structure that sits inside the broader R1 trillion+ commercial property stock regulated by SAPOA industry standards and the PPRA practitioner licensing framework.

- **Global-brand houses** — JLL SA, CBRE, Knight Frank SA, and Cushman &amp; Wakefield (which operates in SA via a partnership with Broll). These firms handle the highest-value international transactions, multinational tenant-rep mandates, and institutional-grade investment sales.
- **Indie heavyweights** — Broll Property Group (founded 1975, Cushman &amp; Wakefield EMEA partner), Galetti Corporate Real Estate, and API Property Group. Broll is widely regarded as the largest SA-headquartered commercial property services firm, with services spanning auctions and sales, facilities management, industrial, investment and office broking, occupier services, property management, retail leasing, research, shopping centre management, and valuations. Galetti (led by MD Cameron Smith) is positioned as one of SA&apos;s leading office and industrial real estate consultancies.
- **Regional and niche specialists** — SAPOA-affiliated indie firms operating in specific sub-markets (Johannesburg CBD, Sandton, Pretoria East, Durban, Cape Town CBD, Ekurhuleni industrial).
- **REIT in-house leasing teams** — Growthpoint Properties, Redefine Properties, Vukile Property Fund, Hyprop Investments, Fortress Real Estate, Attacq, Equites. The JSE-listed REIT sector operates its own leasing and investment teams parallel to the external brokerage market, representing both a counterparty pool for brokers and a direct competitor for tenant-rep mandates.

### Regulation

- **SAPOA (South African Property Owners Association)** — the industry body for commercial property owners and operators. SAPOA publishes the quarterly Office Vacancy Report, Retail Trends, and Industrial Property Report that form the de facto industry benchmark.
- **PPRA (Property Practitioners Regulatory Authority)** — successor to the Estate Agency Affairs Board under the Property Practitioners Act 22 of 2019. All commercial property brokers must hold a valid **Fidelity Fund Certificate (FFC)** to operate.
- **FICA** — commercial property transactions above defined thresholds trigger anti-money-laundering reporting obligations.

## 2. Sub-sectors — office, industrial, retail, investment sales, valuations

Commercial property brokerage in SA operates across six distinct sub-sectors, each with its own economics, cycle timing, and brokerage revenue model.

| Sub-sector | Typical mandate | Revenue model | Cycle timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Office leasing** | 3–10 year leases on A-grade / P-grade buildings | Landlord-paid commission, typically 10–15% of first year&apos;s rent | Tenant demand + remote-work trend |
| **Industrial / warehousing** | 5–15 year leases + sale-and-leaseback | Landlord-paid commission + per-sqm fees on build-to-suit | Logistics corridor growth |
| **Retail leasing** | Anchor tenants + line shops in malls | Landlord-paid, base + turnover rent | Consumer confidence + REIT re-tenanting cycle |
| **Investment sales** | R50m–R2bn+ income-producing properties | Seller-paid commission, 1.5–3% of sale value | Yield compression cycle + REIT capital recycling |
| **Tenant representation** | Corporate occupier side, national or multi-site | Occupier-paid fee or landlord-paid commission split | Corporate headcount / footprint decisions |
| **Valuations** | Independent valuations for bank lending, sale, audit | Fee-based, R10k–R500k+ per valuation | Continuous, cycle-agnostic |

**VRL observation**: the office and investment sales sub-sectors are the broker revenue backbone at the premium end, while retail and industrial provide steadier volume. Tenant representation is the most strategic sub-sector for a signal engine because corporate occupiers generate detectable intent signals (headcount surges, expansion announcements, funding rounds) months before they approach a broker.

## 3. Office return — the first positive CRE cycle since 2019

The most significant recent macro shift in SA commercial property is the **office return cycle**, which has produced the first sustained positive office leasing momentum since the onset of COVID-19.

- **Sandton and Rosebank vacancy declined through 2024–2025** as corporate occupiers re-committed to physical headquarters and expanded footprints beyond the COVID-era minimums.
- **A-grade and P-grade office demand** has outpaced B-grade and C-grade, producing a widening spread between premium and secondary stock — a flight-to-quality dynamic that mirrors global office markets.
- **Hybrid work as the new normal** has stabilised at a level that still requires substantial office space, but with different specifications (more collaboration zones, fewer dedicated desks, more meeting-room density).
- **Sandton** remains the single most important office sub-market in SA by rental value and by transaction volume, followed by Rosebank, Waterfall (Midrand), Century City, Cape Town CBD, and Umhlanga.

**VRL thesis**: the office return cycle is calendar-driven at corporate planning levels. CFOs and HR directors conduct annual space reviews in the August-November window, triggering broker engagement in January-March of the following year. A signal engine that detects corporate headcount and expansion events in the August-November window has a 3-5 month lead time on the eventual broker enquiry.

## 4. Ekurhuleni and the East Rand warehousing boom

The second major structural shift in SA commercial property is the **industrial and warehousing boom in Ekurhuleni and the East Rand**, driven by the logistics corridor between Johannesburg, the Durban port, and the OR Tambo International Airport cargo complex.

- **Ekurhuleni industrial demand** has outpaced office demand through 2024–2025, driven by e-commerce fulfilment growth, the relocation of warehousing from congested Johannesburg central to the East Rand corridor, and the ongoing build-out of last-mile distribution networks.
- **Last-mile distribution nodes** in Kempton Park, Edenvale, Isando, Boksburg, Germiston and Alberton have absorbed significant new industrial lease activity.
- **Equites Property Fund (JSE: EQU)** is a pure-play logistics REIT that has concentrated aggressively on this theme, acquiring and developing prime logistics assets across the corridor.
- **Build-to-suit and sale-and-leaseback** activity has picked up as corporate logistics occupiers prefer to deploy capital into core operations rather than owning the real estate.
- **Warehouse rental rates** have firmed through 2024-2025 relative to office rents, shifting yield compression toward the industrial sub-sector.

**VRL thesis**: warehousing intent is the single most detectable corporate signal in commercial property. Expansion announcements, logistics RFPs, and headcount surges at e-commerce and 3PL companies all precede warehouse lease decisions by 3–6 months. A signal engine that captures these early indicators is positioned to deliver warm tenant-rep leads into the industrial sub-market.

## 5. The JSE REIT counterparty layer

The JSE-listed REIT sector is simultaneously a counterparty pool for brokers and a direct competitor in the tenant-rep and investment sales markets.

- **Growthpoint Properties (JSE: GRT)** &mdash; the largest SA-focused REIT by portfolio value, with office, retail and industrial assets nationwide. Growthpoint&apos;s in-house leasing team is one of the largest broker counterparties in the SA office market.
- **Redefine Properties (JSE: RDF)** &mdash; second-largest SA-focused REIT, with major retail, office and industrial portfolios. Active in office leasing and retail re-tenanting cycles.
- **Vukile Property Fund (JSE: VKE)** &mdash; retail-focused REIT with South African and Iberian portfolios.
- **Hyprop Investments (JSE: HYP)** &mdash; premium retail REIT (Canal Walk, Rosebank Mall, Hyde Park Corner, Woodlands Boulevard).

## 6. Cap rates + yield compression 2025

Commercial property yields ("cap rates") are the single most important pricing signal in the investment sales sub-sector. Yields compressed modestly through 2025 in line with the SARB rate-cut cycle, particularly in the logistics and A-grade office segments.

- **A-grade Sandton office yields** tightened modestly as leasing recovery improved the income security of premium office assets.
- **Prime logistics yields** in Equites-tier assets compressed as institutional appetite for the sub-sector intensified.
- **Retail yields** diverged sharply between premium destination malls (Hyprop-tier) and secondary community centres, with premium retail showing modest tightening and secondary retail remaining under pressure.
- **B-grade and C-grade office yields** remained high (wide), reflecting the flight-to-quality dynamic.
- **Industrial yields** generally tightened more than office yields, reflecting the East Rand corridor demand story.

**VRL caveat**: cap rate figures circulating in trade press are typically **indicative ranges** rather than transaction-specific prints. Any paper quoting a specific cap rate should source it from a specific SAPOA report, a specific REIT SENS release, or a specific brokerage valuation &mdash; not from general market commentary.

## 7. Recent significant moves (2025 → early 2026)

- **Office return cycle** &mdash; the first sustained positive office leasing momentum since 2019, concentrated in Sandton, Rosebank and Waterfall.
- **Ekurhuleni warehousing boom** &mdash; continued absorption of new industrial lease activity across the East Rand corridor.
- **Equites Property Fund** continued to deploy capital into prime logistics assets, reinforcing its pure-play positioning.
- **Growthpoint and Redefine** reported stable-to-improving office occupancy through 2024&ndash;2025.
- **Hyprop portfolio refinements** in the retail sub-sector, with continued focus on premium destination malls.
- **REIT capital recycling** &mdash; several REITs disposed of non-core assets to fund redevelopment of core holdings, increasing investment sales volume.
- **Waterfall City (Attacq)** continued precinct build-out, consolidating the Midrand corridor as a tertiary office and mixed-use hub.
- **Office-to-residential conversion activity** continued in Johannesburg CBD and Pretoria CBD, though at modest volumes relative to the total B-grade and C-grade office stock.
- **PPRA compliance enforcement** continued through 2024&ndash;2025, with FFC checks becoming more systematic.
